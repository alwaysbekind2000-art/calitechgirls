How It All Started
From Home Kitchen to Southern California Icon

It started simply: Chef Keke Chanel Hardy cooking Caribbean food for family and friends in Moreno Valley. No restaurant, no storefront ‚Äî just authentic recipes passed down through generations, made with love in her kitchen.

Word spread the way it always does with great food: person to person, plate to plate. Friends told friends. Neighbors started requesting plates. The demand was undeniable. Keke's International Food Spot was born.

The formula was simple ‚Äî real Caribbean ingredients, slow-cooked with care, served late when everyone else is closed. Oxtail braised for 8 hours. Jerk chicken marinated for 24. Rice & peas made fresh with coconut milk and thyme. Nothing rushed, nothing fake.

Today, 125+ five-star reviews across Moreno Valley and Long Beach tell the story. Orange County is next. The Grand Reopening in March 2026 marks a new chapter ‚Äî bigger, bolder, and more Caribbean than ever.
